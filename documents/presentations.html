Presentations

Documents are often prepped for a presentation so here are some tips:

  • People signal in a variety of ways. For instance, if you have a question and answer section, people can let you know they have a question by raising their hand, speaking their name, or otherwise getting attention.
  • Allow time for people to ask questions. When you open the floor for questions, wait a good amount of time. People need time to process this switch and prepare any questions.
  • Provide printed material in print, large print, and braille.
  • Circulate your presentation materials digitally before the event.
  • If you have visual materials or demonstrations provide verbal descriptions of them. If you present a lot of slideshows, get into the habit of describing things. For example, "The image on the slide is our team photo from last year's retreat." Or if you have a chart, summarize.
  • Make sure anything on the screen is spoken or shared.
  • Check the aucoustics of the space, use a microphone, and to speak directly into it.
  • Arrange CART and ASL when needed.
  • Start a presentation with an access check. Ask participants if there are any issues with the setup. I'll say something like: "Just to do a quick access check: is my volume okay, am I speaking at an okay pace?"
